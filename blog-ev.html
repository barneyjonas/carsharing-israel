Electric cars in Israeli carsharing — what to know

Which providers offer EVs, why it matters, and what to check before you book.

Not all carsharing providers in Israel offer electric vehicles — and it matters more than you might think. Here is what to know before you book.

Why EV carsharing makes sense

The usual barrier to driving electric is home charging infrastructure. Carsharing removes that barrier entirely: the provider charges the vehicles, and you just book and drive. You get the benefits — zero tailpipe emissions, quieter ride, lower running costs — without installing anything at home.

In dense Israeli cities where air quality and parking are already strained, EV carsharing is also a meaningful environmental step. One shared electric car can replace multiple privately-owned petrol cars.

AutoTel: Israel's EV carsharing leader

AutoTel operates a free-floating fleet in Tel Aviv-Yafo that is primarily electric and hybrid. This is the only provider in Israel where you are likely to get an EV by default, without specifically requesting one. The free-floating model — pick up and drop off anywhere within the service zone — makes it ideal for short city errands.

What to check before booking an EV

  • Battery range: Most carsharing EVs are charged and ready, but check the app before booking — some providers show current charge level.
  • Return rules: You do not need to charge the car before returning it. The provider handles charging.
  • Service zone: Free-floating EVs (AutoTel) must be returned within the service zone — dropping outside it incurs a fee.
  • App registration: Register in advance. EV bookings through AutoTel require the same setup as standard vehicles: ID verification and a credit card.
