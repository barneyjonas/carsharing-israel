Frequently asked questions

Common questions about carsharing in Israel — costs, eligibility, tourist use, and how different services work.

What is carsharing?

Carsharing lets you use a car for short periods — hours or a day — without owning one. You pay only for what you use: time and sometimes distance. No insurance, maintenance, or parking costs.

How is carsharing different from car rental?

Car rental typically requires a branch visit, daily billing, and more paperwork. Carsharing is app-based, faster to access, and often priced per minute or hour. It's designed for short, spontaneous trips.

Do I need an Israeli ID to use carsharing?

Most Israeli providers require an Israeli ID and driving licence. Tourist eligibility is currently unverified for most providers — check directly with each provider before booking.

What is the minimum age?

Typically 21 years old, sometimes 24 for certain vehicle categories. This varies by provider — always verify on their official website or app.

What if the car is damaged?

You are usually covered by the provider's insurance with a deductible (השתתפות עצמית). Some providers offer deductible reduction packages. Read the terms carefully before you start.

Can tourists use carsharing in Israel?

Potentially yes, but it is not guaranteed. Requirements vary by provider and include: a valid foreign licence, sometimes an International Driving Permit (IDP), a credit card, and minimum age. Always contact the provider directly.

Is carsharing cheaper than owning a car?

It can be, but there is no universal mileage threshold. Compare your depreciation, insurance, registration, parking, fuel and maintenance with the time, distance, membership and insurance charges for your actual trips.

What is free-floating carsharing?

Free-floating (area-based) means you can pick up and drop off a car anywhere within a defined service zone. AutoTel in Tel Aviv uses this model. No fixed parking spot required.

What is round-trip carsharing?

You pick up a car at a designated station or spot and return it to the same or another designated location. GoTo, CityCar, and My Car use this model.

How do I return the car?

Depends on the service type. Free-floating: park within the service zone. Round-trip: return to the designated station. Check the provider's app before ending your trip — returning incorrectly can incur fees.

Do I need an app?

Yes — all current Israeli carsharing providers are app-based. Download and register before you need a car, as registration can take time (ID verification, credit card, etc.).

Are there electric vehicles (EVs)?

My Car publishes a BYD electric category, GoTo's tariff refers to electric vehicles, and Toyota SHARE lists models that may be hybrid depending on availability. AutoTel's public sources do not establish a current electric fleet. Use the provider links and confirm the exact vehicle in the app.
