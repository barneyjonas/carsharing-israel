How carsharing works in Israel

A neutral guide for understanding different shared-car models before choosing a provider.

1. What counts as carsharing?

For this MVP, carsharing means app-based or self-service access to a car for short periods without owning the vehicle. The category is broader than classic station-based sharing and includes some short-term rental-like services.

2. Carsharing vs. traditional rental

Traditional rental usually depends on branches, paperwork and daily rental logic. Carsharing usually focuses on faster access, shorter trips, and cars located closer to residential or urban areas.

3. Round-trip carsharing

You pick up a car at a defined place and usually return it to the same or another designated place. This is often useful for errands, planned trips and households without a private car.

4. Free-floating / area-based access

You can end the trip within a defined operating area. This is useful for short city trips, but it depends heavily on the exact service area and parking rules.

5. Short-term app rental

Some services are not classic carsharing but still solve a similar user problem: flexible access to a vehicle without a full rental-counter process.

6. What to check before booking

  • Do you need an Israeli ID or local licence?
  • Is insurance included and what is the deductible?
  • Are fuel, charging, parking and mileage included?
  • Can you use it on Shabbat or holidays?
  • Where exactly can you return the vehicle?

7. Common cost factors

Costs can include time, distance, subscription, deductible reduction, late return, cleaning, parking, charging or fuel rules. Exact pricing needs provider-level verification.

8. What this MVP does not do yet

It does not provide live availability, exact prices, booking, payment, vehicle unlock, maps or TOMP-API integration. It is a structured overview and comparison layer.

Visiting Israel? What tourists need to know

Carsharing in Israel is primarily designed for residents. Tourist eligibility varies by provider and is not fully verified for most services. Here is what to check before you book.

1. Driving licence

Most providers require a valid local or international driving licence. A valid foreign licence may be accepted — but this is unverified for most Israeli carsharing services. An International Driving Permit (IDP) alongside your national licence is the safest approach.

2. Which nationalities can drive in Israel?

Israel recognises driving licences from most countries for tourists staying up to 90 days. EU, US, UK, Australian, and Canadian licences are generally accepted. Always verify with the specific provider — their insurance terms may differ from general road rules.

3. Credit card requirement

All Israeli carsharing providers require a credit card for registration and deposit. Debit cards and prepaid cards may not be accepted. The deposit amount varies by provider and vehicle type.

4. What to check before booking

  • Does the provider accept your nationality and licence?
  • Is an International Driving Permit (IDP) required?
  • What is the minimum age (often 21–24)?
  • Is a local Israeli ID or phone number required for registration?
  • Does the insurance cover tourists?
